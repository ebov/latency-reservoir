# Ebola evolution between outbreaks

It has long been thought that Ebola under went a genetic bottleneck near the 1970s 
after which it spread out of the North of the DRC as an active outbreak, or chain of 
outbreaks in an unobserved reservoir. During this time period, Ebola caused periodic
clusters of outbreaks in humans and nonhuman mammals. 

This model is largely predicated on a  root position that places the common ancestor 
of all Ebola diversity near the first sequenced cases. More recent sequences are 
inconsistent with this model. However, the model has long been accepted 
and recent observations have been made to fit.
In this section we will explore the statistical evidence for this rooting 
in the original datasets used to support it.
